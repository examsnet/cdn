158. (d) An electron withdrawing group like $\mathrm{NO}_{2}, \mathrm{Br}$ and $\mathrm{Ph}$ increases the acidity of carboxylic acid. It actually stabilises the negative charge present on the carboxylate ion by inductive/resonance effect. The order of electron withdrawing substituent present in the given carboxylic acid is $\mathrm{CH}_{3}<\mathrm{Ph}-<\mathrm{Br}-<\mathrm{NO}_{2}$-. Thus, the order of acidity is $\mathrm{A}<\mathrm{B}<\mathrm{C}<\mathrm{D}$.

159. (d) The correct match is $A \rightarrow$ (III), $B \rightarrow$ (I), $\mathrm{C} \rightarrow$ (II).

Formic acid is found in many ants sting. Acetic acid can be obtained from vinegar and butyric acid is present in butter.

160. (b) The electron withdrawing group like $-\mathrm{CH}_{3}$, $-\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{H}_{5}$ etc. increases the basicity when present in a compound. Thus, (a) is most acidic among the given compounds as it has two $-\mathrm{C}_{2} \mathrm{H}_{5}$ group, then comes (b) as it has one - $\mathrm{CH}_{3}$ group, then comes $\mathrm{NH}_{3}$. Aniline is least basic as its lone pair of electrons are involve in resonance. Thus, the basicity order is as follows $\mathrm{A}>\mathrm{B}>\mathrm{C}>\mathrm{D}$.
